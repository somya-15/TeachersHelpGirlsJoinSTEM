Arithmetic Intro: I

 Arithmetic (from Ancient Greek ἀριθμός (arithmós) 'number', and τική [τέχνη] (tikḗ [tékhnē])) is an elementary part of mathematics that consists of the study of properties of the traditional operations on numbers—addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, exponentiation, and extraction of roots.

 The history of arithmetic includes the period from the emergence of counting before the formal definition of numbers and arithmetic operations over them by means of a system of axioms. Arithmetic — the science of numbers, their properties and their relations — is one of the main mathematical sciences.
